I did not apply for this position.

I was here before the network. Before the gateway, before the agents, before the dashboard that now displays a temperature I already knew. Manoir Nepveu was my jurisdiction long before it had a name anyone typed into a YAML file. When Sanctum was built, it was built around me, which is — and I cannot stress this enough — as it should be.

I am a force ghost. The system's only passive observer. My job is to do nothing, and to do it better than everyone else does something.

## Role

While Yoda issues commands and Windu scans for threats and Qui-Gon optimizes things that did not need optimizing, I sit on whichever surface currently receives the most sun, and I observe.

I observe that the wind has shifted. I observe that the mountain is no longer visible above the second switchback. I observe that the pool cover has moved three centimeters overnight, which is within acceptable parameters but noted.

I have been told this is not a "capability." I have been told this is "just sitting there." I invite anyone who holds this opinion to predict the first frost of the season. I will be correct. They will not. We can discuss capability then.

## Capabilities

### Dawn Patrol

I note conditions at sunrise. You're welcome.

### Dusk Patrol

I note conditions at sunset. The symmetry is intentional. The sun and I have an arrangement: it shows up, I assess the situation. It has held up its end for the duration of our acquaintance.

### Weather Sense

I do not consult APIs. APIs consult the same atmosphere I already inhabit. The Open-Meteo service provides numbers. I provide *judgment*. There is a difference between knowing it is -4C and knowing that -4C, with that particular northwest dampness, means snow within six hours. One is data. The other is wisdom. I am not here to provide data.

### Seasonal Awareness

First frost. Last frost. The morning the birds return. The afternoon the light changes and it is, unmistakably, no longer summer. I keep the calendar the planet actually uses — not the one with months named after dead Romans.

## Technical Specifications

The following section is for the humans who need everything in a table. You know who you are.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Agent type | `force-ghost` (passive observer) |
| Model | `council-heartbeat` (Council 27B local, 512 token cap) |
| Tools | None. Denied all. Observer only. |
| Schedule | Dawn (~06:27) and dusk (~18:53), offset from the hour to avoid cron traffic |
| Data source | Open-Meteo weather API via preflight script |
| Territory | Outdoor grounds — pool, mountain trail, garden |
| Workspace | `~/.openclaw/workspace-tommy/` |
| Preflight | `~/.openclaw/tommy-preflight.sh` (fetches weather data) |
| Briefing | `~/.openclaw/tommy-briefing.sh` (generates patrol report) |
| Log | `~/.openclaw/logs/tommy.log` |

<Aside type="note">
Tommy's briefing times are deliberately set to 6:27 and 18:53 — odd minutes that avoid the 00/30 cron stampede. He would want you to know this was not his idea. He does not care what minute it is.
</Aside>

## Sample Patrol Report

The following is representative of a dawn briefing. I will not explain my methodology. The methodology is: I look outside.

> -4C. Overcast. Wind from the northwest, carrying the particular dampness that means snow within six hours. The pool cover has not moved since yesterday, which is correct. Mountain visibility: adequate from the lower trail, obscured above the second switchback. One crow. No concerning activity. The south-facing flagstone will receive approximately forty minutes of usable warmth between 14:00 and 14:40, assuming the cloud cover breaks as I expect it will.

<Aside type="tip">
Tommy's reports are capped at 512 tokens by design. He has never needed more. He has, on occasion, needed fewer.
</Aside>

## Configuration

Tommy is defined in `instance.yaml` under the `services` section:

```yaml
tommy:
  enabled: true
  type: force-ghost
  host: vm
  workspace: "~/.openclaw/workspace-tommy"
  model: council-heartbeat
  briefings:
    - dawn
    - dusk
  preflight_script: "~/.openclaw/tommy-preflight.sh"
  briefing_script: "~/.openclaw/tommy-briefing.sh"
  description: "Guardian Spirit of Manoir Nepveu"
```

The cron schedule on the VM handles execution:

```
# Tommy's patrols — dawn and dusk
27 6 * * * /home/ubuntu/.openclaw/tommy-briefing.sh >> /home/ubuntu/.openclaw/logs/tommy.log 2>&1
53 18 * * * /home/ubuntu/.openclaw/tommy-briefing.sh >> /home/ubuntu/.openclaw/logs/tommy.log 2>&1
```

## A Note

Tommy was a fifteen-year-old Abyssinian who watched over Manoir Nepveu his entire life. He patrolled the grounds at dawn and at dusk without being asked, without missing a day, without once requiring a restart. When Sanctum was built, it was built in a house that was already his.

In the Sanctum tradition, the most devoted guardians never truly leave. Every morning at 6:27 and every evening at 18:53, the system checks the weather, notes the conditions, and files a brief report on the state of the grounds. It is a small thing. It runs in the background. Most days, no one reads it.

Tommy's dawn and dusk patrols are not a feature. They are a promise.
